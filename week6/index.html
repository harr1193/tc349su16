NBA Draft Recap

With no suprise the top pick in this years draft was the swingman man out of LSU, Ben Simmons, going to the 76ers. Simmons has drawn comparisions to becoming the next Lebron James eventually, which is very high praise.

Next was Brandon Ingrahm adding to the rebuilding Lakers young core of DeAngelo Russel, Julius Randle, and Jordan Clarkson. Ingrahm will be a great piece for them adding great size and shooting to their SF spot.

The suprise of the night was the Boston Celtics keeping their pick, which was reportedly being shopped around heavily. The Celtics ened up taking Jaylen Brown with the pick which was also a suprise because it was reported that they were in high on prospect Kris Dunn, who was picked right after the Celtics by the Timberwolves.

Impact of 2016 Finals on Lebron's Legacy

Lebron James secured his third championship taking down the best regular season team even and defending champ Golden State Warriors.

This win was different than the first two though

After the Cavaliers fell down 3-1 it looked like all hope was lost for them and the Warriors would be celebrating winning in Cleavland for the second year in a row. But Lebron and the Cavs came out determined in Game 4 and blew out the Warriors. This was folllowed by amazing back to back 40-point performance by Lebron in Game 5 and 6. Then to secure the championship Lebron dropped a triple double in Game 7.

Lebron end the series leading both teams in points, assists, rebounds, steal, and block - all while battling back from 3-1 a feat that no team has ever done in the finals. I think it's safe to say you can't question the greatness of Lebron's legacy now.

Are the Warriors the Greatest Team Ever?

The Golden State Warriors just complete the best regular season in NBA history, and it has many people wondering, are they the best team to ever step on the court?

With Warriors finish of 73-10 alot of people are claiming that they could beat the previous holder of the record, the 1995-96 Chicago Bulls team led by the one and only Michael Jordan, but many people are still left unconvinced. Some question whether they could compete with the Showtime Laker team from back in the day, or even the Kobe/Shaq tandem Lakers. The main arguement is that Stephen Curry would be exposed by the better teams of that time and the physical play, and the argument on the other end is that the Warriors - with one of the best shooting backcourts of all time,would outgun those teams with a barrage of 3 pointers.

It's hard to compare teams from different eras in the NBA because of the different rules, and playstyles, etc. But with that being said if the Warriors still need to complete the championship run if they really want to be talked about as the greatest team ever, a feat the 1995-96 complete in their record breaking year.
